## Page 3

**Root cause: proving is expensive, validating is expensive, and the two sides use different standards**

It is not that the entity is not green. The problem is that making green claims acceptable to markets and regulators is too expensive and too inconsistent.

- Submitter side: enterprises, asset developers, lenders
- Acceptor side: brands, customs, asset buyers, banks
- Green proof and green acceptance use different standards
- Methodology compliance and evidence-chain compliance often diverge
- Proof generation is mostly manual
- Acceptance rules, risk review, and transaction status are also mostly manual
- Typical timeline: 3-12 months
- Typical cost: $5k-$300k for proof, $5k-$400k for acceptance
- Result: repeated recalculation, repeated review, failed trades or financing
- Around 80% of assets remain locked from circulation

## Page 4

The same structure repeats across carbon compliance, carbon assets/RWA, and green finance:
submitter proof -> acceptor decision -> compliance, funding, or transaction flow.

- Carbon compliance: 1.4 million enterprises, 2 billion product batches
- Carbon assets/RWA and allowances: 1 billion tons of inventory, 10,000+ participating enterprises
- Green finance/RWA: about EUR0.8T in loans and bonds
- Buyers decide procurement, tax cost, and market access
- Exchanges and buyers decide trading success and pricing
- Banks and investors decide loan size and interest rate
- Large external consulting, development, and assessment markets already exist around these decisions
